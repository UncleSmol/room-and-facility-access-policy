Room and Facility Access Policy for Students

Purpose

This policy defines the guidelines for Nkangala TVET College students' access to rooms and facilities at Villa Roma Hotel, balancing educational needs with guest privacy, safety, and operational integrity. The goal is to provide students with hands-on learning opportunities while ensuring guest spaces remain private, secure, and undisturbed.

Scope

This policy applies to all students and trainees from Nkangala TVET College participating in training at Villa Roma Hotel. It includes access to guest rooms, public areas, back-of-house facilities, and other operational spaces necessary for training purposes.

Definitions

  • Student/Trainee: A student from Nkangala TVET College who is part of a hands-on training program at Villa Roma Hotel.
  • Authorized Access: Areas or rooms that students are permitted to enter as part of their training activities under the supervision of hotel staff.
  • Restricted Areas: Rooms and facilities that are off-limits to students to protect guest privacy and ensure safety.
  • Back-of-House Areas: Non-guest areas where students may be allowed for training purposes, such as the kitchen, laundry room, and housekeeping storage areas.

Facility Access Guidelines

General Access Principles

  • Supervised Access: Students may only enter guest rooms and back-of-house areas under the direct supervision of a hotel supervisor, mentor, or authorized staff member.
  • Purpose-Driven Access: Access to any facility should be for training purposes only and align with Villa Roma's educational objectives.
  • Respect for Privacy: At all times, students must respect guest privacy by refraining from entering occupied guest rooms and respecting the confidentiality of guest information.

Guest Rooms

  • Authorized Access: Students may only enter vacant or training-designated rooms under supervision to practice room service, housekeeping, and room setup skills.
  • Occupied Rooms: Under no circumstances may students enter rooms occupied by guests, even if accompanied by a supervisor, to ensure privacy and avoid disruptions.
  • Housekeeping Training: Students may access designated training rooms to learn housekeeping procedures, including cleaning, linen changes, and room checks. All actions must be supervised to ensure compliance with Villa Roma standards.

Public Areas

  • Lobby and Reception: Students are allowed in the lobby and reception area for training in front desk operations, guest interactions, and concierge services, under supervision.
  • Dining Areas: Students may access the dining areas for training in food service, including setup, serving, and clearing. This access is limited to non-peak hours or under designated training sessions.
  • Recreational Facilities: Access to recreational facilities, such as the pool area or fitness centre, is generally restricted. However, students may receive guided training in these areas on facility management or guest assistance during scheduled sessions.

Back-of-House Areas

  • Kitchen: Students may access the kitchen during designated training sessions for food preparation, safety, and hygiene training, under the supervision of kitchen staff or mentors.
  • Laundry Room: Access is permitted for housekeeping and laundry training sessions, where students learn procedures for linen handling and laundry services.
  • Storage Areas: Students may enter housekeeping storage areas to learn about supply management, inventory practices, and stocking procedures, under staff supervision.
  • Staff Lounges: Access to staff lounges is limited to designated break times and only when accompanied by a supervisor. Students should not remain in these areas unless on an approved break.

Restricted Areas

  • Guest-Only Facilities: Areas such as the spa, gym, and business centre are generally off-limits to students unless they are engaged in specific training activities approved by management.
  • Executive Offices: Access to hotel management offices is restricted to authorized personnel only. Students may only enter these areas if invited for specific training or meetings with a supervisor or mentor.
  • Security and Maintenance Rooms: Access to security or maintenance control rooms is strictly prohibited for students to ensure guest safety and operational security.

Access Procedures and Protocols

Check-In and Check-Out for Access

  • Sign-In: Students must sign in with the front desk or their supervisor before entering any back-of-house area or guest room for training. This ensures accurate tracking of access and presence in sensitive areas.
  • Check-Out: Students must check out or log their exit from each area after completing their training tasks. This helps supervisors account for all students' activities.

Key and Access Card Protocol

  • Supervised Key Access: Students will not be issued access keys or cards. Authorized staff members will use their keys to grant students supervised entry to restricted areas.
  • Prohibited Key Use: Students are prohibited from handling room keys, master keys, or access cards. Supervisors are responsible for opening and securing all areas as required for training.

Attire and Identification

  • Uniform Requirement: Students must wear their designated uniforms when accessing any guest-facing or back-of-house areas for training.
  • Identification: Students must display their trainee identification badge at all times, clearly indicating their role to both staff and guests.

Privacy and Confidentiality Standards

Guest Privacy and Data Protection

  • Non-Disclosure of Guest Information: Students must not discuss or disclose any information related to guests, including room numbers, booking details, or personal preferences.
  • Respect for Personal Belongings: While in guest rooms or storage areas, students should not touch or handle any guest belongings and must report any found items to a supervisor.

Confidentiality Agreement

  • Agreement to Confidentiality: All students are required to sign a confidentiality agreement, affirming their commitment to upholding guest privacy and maintaining confidentiality with any information encountered during training.
  • Reporting Breaches: Any unintentional breach of guest privacy or confidentiality should be reported immediately to a supervisor to ensure appropriate corrective actions are taken.

Supervisory Responsibilities

Monitoring Student Access

  • Direct Supervision: Supervisors must accompany students in any guest room, back-of-house area, or restricted public area during training.
  • Training Oversight: Supervisors are responsible for providing clear instructions on access limitations, enforcing policy adherence, and ensuring students respect guest and hotel property.

Addressing Policy Violations

  • Corrective Action: Any student who fails to adhere to this policy may face corrective action, which could include restricted access, additional training, or, for severe violations, removal from the training program.
  • Documentation of Violations: Supervisors must document all access-related violations and report them to the training manager or HR department.

Policy Review and Updates

This Room and Facility Access Policy for Students will be reviewed annually to ensure it meets the needs of Villa Roma Hotel's operational standards and training requirements. Any updates will be communicated to all relevant staff and trainees.

Acknowledgment

All students, supervisors, and mentors must review and acknowledge this policy upon the start of each training program, confirming their understanding of room and facility access rules and their commitment to guest privacy and operational integrity.
